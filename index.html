The Problem

Homeowners don't land on your site ready to talk sales. They land with questions—and your website usually can't answer them. So they leave.

Problem #1

Stop Scaring Homeowners Away

Most solar sites push visitors straight into a contact form. Some are worse—they ask for a zip code to "provide an estimate," then hit the homeowner with "we'll call you to discuss next steps."

To a homeowner, that feels like a bait-and-switch sales pitch—not help.

Problem #2

Stop Losing Qualified Leads

Whether visitors come from Google, Yelp, referrals, or paid ads—if they leave without understanding if solar works for their home, you've lost them. They bounce. You get nothing.

The competitor who answered their questions first gets the appointment.
